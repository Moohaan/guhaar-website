BELIEFS

  • The poor and downtrodden have as much of a right to be heard as the privileged sections.
  • Journalism should be inclusive and not based on financial gains, so that the poorer section of the society can make their voices heard.
  • Rural population constitutes about 70% of the total Indian population and hence, deserves much more than the present 5 percent media coverage.
  • Promoting a culture of accountability. Only by asking questions will we get the answers we deserve.
  • Acting as a bridge between the downtrodden masses and their better off fellow citizen.

WHAT WE DO

Here's what we have included in our objectives for Guhaar. We will keep adding and improving these as needed.

  • Provide media consultancy services to social movements. For this purpose, an introductory article and a documentary will be made about the issue and events which happened in the affected areas. This helps spread awareness about the problem among the general public. Then a regular update will be done on the website to give people all the necessary real, transparent information regarding the on-going issues. The localities will also be aware about the value and importance of social media, parallel to the ground level fight.
  • The RTI wing of Guhaar will collect the data regarding the issue. In this way, reliable information and data will be collected which can be utilised further.
  • A letter writing wing will write letters to various officials to make them aware of the issues concerned, present scenario of the locality and to share a model for a solution.
  • The fact-finding team will coordinate with the local organisations and people to get reliable information about the issue.
  • A thorough assessment of the most popular and non-popular issues of the country will be done and critical reviews, views of experts and reality of the situation will be presented to people through our portal. Regular updates will also be given on our portal.

Guhaar partners with various local as well as national level organisations related to social movements and a group of socialists and activists who are not politically biased, so as to get realistic data that will help paint a true picture of the situation.

EXPOSURE

Guhaar provides an opportunity to young and enthusiastic people,

  • Who feel that the existing system needs improvement,
  • Who are willing to work for the betterment of the society,
  • Willing to change the conventional definition of government by asking questions and helping it stay true to the principles of democracy and also to battle on behalf of those unprivileged, helpless, downtrodden citizens of the India who cannot win their fight alone.
  • Along with creating the impact on the society, it is equally necessary to do value addition to oneself.   • By being in this space, one will get ample chances to work with the organisations and movements working at the grassroots level.
  • One will learn how to manage unorganized groups and also other traits such as,
    • How to be disciplined
    • How to address the people
    • Managing events
    • Speaking to a variety of people ranging from rural people to renowned activists and government officials,
    • How it feels working for a social cause
    • The ground realities and a chance to build the Incredible India of their dreams.

Besides this, working with Guhaar also gives an enormous opportunity to have healthy discussions on the problems our country is facing, understanding the power of journalism and in understanding the impact of RTI. In nutshell, these days many of us have a lot of anger towards the system and this space provides one of the ways to channelize our anger in a way that can help bring meaningful changes in the society. After all, if not us, then who?
