BELIEFS

  1. Poor and downtrodden has as much of a right to be heard as the privileged.
  2. Journalism should be inclusive and non-monetary which do not eliminate the poorer section of the society.
  3. Being 70 percent of the total population in India, the rural population deserves much more than present 5 percent media coverage.
  4. Developing the culture of asking questions.
  5. Creating a bridge between affected and non-affected mass.

WORKSPACE

Here's what has tentatively been included in the work-space of the Guhaar. We will keep adding according to our needs and as the organisation grows.

  1. To provide media consultancy to the movements. In order to implement, an introductory article and a documentary will be made about the whole issue and the events which happened in the affected areas so as to give an idea about the problems to the general public. Then a regular update will be done on the website to give people all the necessary real, transparent information regarding the on-going issues. The localities will also be aware about the value and importance of social media, parallel to the ground level fight.
  2. The RTI wing of Guhaar will collect the data regarding the issues concerned which need to be focused. In this way, a reliable information and data will be collected which will be presented further.
  3. A letter writing wing will write the letters to various officials to make them aware of the issues concerned, the present scenario of the locality and to give the model of a solution.
  4. The fact-finding team will coordinate with the local organisations and people to get the reliable information about the issue.
  5. A thorough assessment of the most popular and non-popular issues of the country will be done and critical reviews and the reality will be presented to people through our portal. The regular updates will also be given on our portal.

Guhaar is working with various local organisations, national level organisations related to movements and a group of socialists and activists, which are not politically inclined, so to get the reliable data and the real picture of the situation.

EXPOSURE

Guhaar provides an opportunity to young and enthusiastic people,

  1. Who have discontent towards the system
  2. Who are willing to work for the betterment of the society,
  3. Willing to change the conventional definition of government in terms of asking them questions instead of resisting its suppression and also to battle on behalf of those unprivileged, helpless, downtrodden citizens of the India who cannot win their fight alone.
  4. Along with creating the impact on the society, it is equally necessary to do value addition to oneself. In this space, the energetic youth will be provided an opportunity to be a part of the change process and to enhance oneself.
  5. By being in this space, one will get ample chances to work with the organisations and movements working at the grassroots level.
  6. The experience one would get working in such organisations will be totally different.
  7. One will learn how to manage unorganized groups,
    1. How to be disciplined
    2. How to address the people
    3. Managing events
    4. Speaking to a variety of people ranging from rural people to renowned activists and government officials,
    5. How it feels working for the social cause
    6. The ground realities and knowing the picture behind the Incredible India.

Besides this, working with Guhaar also gives an enormous opportunity to have healthy discussions, understanding the power of journalism and to understand the power of RTI. In nutshell, these days all of us have a lot of anger towards the system and this space provides one of the ways to channelize our anger towards the change that we can bring in the society.
